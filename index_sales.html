Weekly Activity Plan
Part-time reps target 5 cold stops + 1 warm follow-up daily. Full-time reps double it.

The quote tool is your unfair advantage. Most competitors can't build and send a branded estimate from their phone. Do it in front of every warm prospect before you leave. Close the loop on the spot.

Mon
Plan the week. Pull 10–15 targets from Google Maps — restaurants, realtors, contractors in Maricopa and Casa Grande. Confirm any follow-ups from last week.
Tue
Cold stop block. Hit 8–12 businesses. Restaurants, salons, retail. Drop a leave-behind. If the owner is present, open the quote tool and build something on the spot.
Wed
Events and orgs day. Sports leagues, churches, school booster clubs, HOAs. Lead with the full package — spirit wear, banners, big heads, canopy tents.
Thu
Corporate and contractor block. Real estate offices, HVAC, plumbing, construction. Lead with tumblers, polos, truck magnets, yard signs. Leave a branded sample kit.
Fri
Warm lead follow-up. Call or text every interested prospect from the week. Send quote links. If a prospect went cold, drop a quick check-in text — no pressure, just presence.
Prospect Tracks
Five segments, each with a different entry point and best ask.
Track 1
Local commercial — restaurants, salons, gyms, retail
  • Best ask: staff shirts, window graphics, event banners, stickers
  • Entry: walk in 2–4pm weekdays. Ask for the owner by name.
  • Trigger line: "How many staff do you have? Let me build a shirt quote right now."
Track 2
Events & orgs — leagues, churches, schools, HOAs
  • Best ask: spirit wear packages, banners, big heads, foam board, canopy tents
  • Entry: athletic director, pastor, event coordinator — email or walk-in
  • Timing: target 8–12 weeks before season start or major event
  • Key pitch: "One vendor, one quote — shirts, banners, signs, and the big heads."
Track 3
Real estate & contractors
  • Best ask: yard signs, truck magnets, polos, tumblers, foam board
  • Entry: office drop-in or Chamber / BNI networking
  • Key pitch: "Your brand is on every job site and every door. We make sure it looks sharp."
  • Upsell path: shirts → signs → Promo with Grace gifting kits
Track 4 — SDVOSB Set-Aside
Government & nonprofit
  • Best ask: branded apparel, event materials, engraved recognition items, promo kits
  • Entry: procurement officer, admin director, or foundation contact
  • Key pitch: "We're SDVOSB certified — you can purchase from us to hit your small business spend goals with less paperwork."
  • Always leave a capability statement + SDVOSB cert copy
Track 5
Corporate accounts — HR, marketing, gifting buyers
  • Best ask: onboarding kits, event swag bundles, Promo with Grace Good/Better/Best packages
  • Entry: office manager, HR, or marketing coordinator
  • Key pitch: "Branded kit for every new hire or every tradeshow — shirts, tumbler, hat, notepad. Want to see tiers?"
  • Highest lifetime value segment. Push toward annual agreements.
Talking Points
Lead with their world. The goal of the first stop is one thing: permission to build a quote.
Universal door opener

"Hey, I'm [name] with Little 6 Industries — we're a local print shop right here in Maricopa. Shirts, signs, banners, branded gear — all in-house, fast turnaround. Do you ever need anything like that for your team or your events?"

If they already have a vendor

"Totally understand — most people do. I'm not here to replace anyone. But if there's ever a rush job, a small run, or something they can't handle, I'd love to be your backup. Mind if I leave a card?"

Transition to the quote tool

"Here — let me pull up our quoting tool and build you an actual estimate right now. Takes two minutes, I'll email it to you before I leave. What do you order most — shirts, signs, or something else?"

Rep Toolkit
What every rep carries every time they're in the field.
01
Mobile quote tool
Your #1 asset. Practice the flow before every stop. Build a quote for every warm prospect before you walk out the door.
02
Capability statement
One page. Business overview, SDVOSB cert, NAICS codes, UEI/CAGE, contact info. Essential for government and corporate calls.
03
Physical sample kit
DTF transfer on a shirt, die-cut sticker, foam board sample, engraved tumbler. Let prospects touch the quality. Leave a sample when the door is warm.
04
Leave-behind card
Branded card with QR code linking to little6llc.com. Top 8–10 products listed. Short, scannable, stays on their desk.
05
Good / Better / Best promo sheet
One-page tiered promo product options from Promo with Grace. Perfect for corporate gifting and event organizers who don't know where to start.
06
L6I branded apparel — on your body
Embroidered polo or DTF-printed shirt, every stop, every day. You are a walking sample. If someone asks where you got the shirt, that's a lead.
Every rep should be able to demo the quote tool cold in under 60 seconds. If they can't, they're not ready to go out.
