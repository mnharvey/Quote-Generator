Trip Overview
Sierra Vista — 5 targets, 3 real business opportunities, 1 personal stop, 1 soft lead. Fort Huachuca market. Military-heavy customer base. SDVOSB angle plays well here.

Lead with the veteran card — everywhere. Sierra Vista is a Fort Huachuca town. 19,000+ military personnel and contractors live and shop here. Every business owner knows that customer. You being SDVOSB certified and a veteran yourself is not a footnote — it's your opening line.

The Mall at Sierra Vista
2200 El Mercado Loop · Sierra Vista, AZ
Only enclosed mall south of Tucson · 50+ stores
Recon
What we know
  • Only indoor mall south of Tucson — draws from 75-mile radius including Mexico border towns
  • Fort Huachuca proximity means heavy military customer base
  • Mix of national chains and local independent tenants
  • Mall has a 1st Saturdays small business market — local vendor community exists here
  • Anchors: Dillard's, Best Buy, Cinemark — independents fill the inline spaces
Opportunity
  • Walk the floor — identify any local independent retailers (not chains)
  • Kiosk and cart operators are often the best targets — lower budget, need signage and shirts fast
  • Food court operators for staff shirts and window graphics
  • Don't pitch to chains — find the owner-operated spots
Strategy: This is a recon stop, not a pitch stop. Spend 20 minutes walking the floor. Identify 3–4 independent businesses. Drop a card and a leave-behind. Follow up by email later that week.

Personalized Pitches
Tailored openers for each stop. Memorize the first two lines before you walk in.

"Hey — I'm Matt Harvey, Little 6 Industries, out of Maricopa. We're a veteran-owned print shop — SDVOSB certified. I heard you guys are the go-to for the military community down here and I wanted to introduce myself. Do you do any branded merch for the store?"

"Your customer base already identifies with this brand. We could put your logo on shirts, hats, stickers — stuff your regulars would actually buy and wear. Let me build you a quick quote right now — takes two minutes."

What to Carry
Pack the night before. Don't leave without all six.
01
Phone with quote tool loaded — practice the flow once in the car before your first stop. Know it cold.
02
Capability statement — printed, 5+ copies. Pull it out at Apocalypse Arms and Sarge's specifically. The SDVOSB cert on there matters to these guys.
03
Sample kit — tailored per stop:

Apocalypse Arms & West-End Pawn: Engraved tumbler or acrylic (they sell branded gear — show them what engraving looks like on a real product). Die-cut sticker in a tactical/military shape if you have one. DTF transfer on a dark shirt — their customers wear dark tactical colors.

Rising Sun Games: Sticker is your hero piece here — gaming culture lives and dies on stickers. Bring your sharpest die-cut. A foam board sample works too — they need in-store signage for events. Show them a shirt transfer on something colorful.

Sarge's Sidearms: Same as Apocalypse Arms — engraved item first, then sticker. Jeff is a Henry dealer — patriot brand, American-made. Bring anything with clean, sharp engraving. That's the product that resonates with this customer.

Mall walk: Bring a little of everything — you don't know who you'll meet. The tumbler and a sticker are your two most universal conversation pieces.

Let them hold it. Let them ask "how much?" That question means you open the quote tool.
04
Leave-behind cards — with a handwritten note per stop:

Apocalypse Arms: Write "veteran-owned, SDVOSB — let's work together" on the back before you walk in. Jim and Kara will keep that card.

Rising Sun Games: Write "store merch + event stickers — let's talk" on the back. Specific enough to remind them exactly what the conversation was about.

Sarge's Sidearms: Address it to Jeff by name. "Jeff — vet to vet. Call anytime." That card lives on his counter.

Mall walk: Straight card, no note — you're moving fast. Quantity over personalization here.

Bring at least 20. Leave one at every stop even if the owner isn't there.
05
Good/Better/Best promo sheet — especially useful at Rising Sun Games and for the mall walk. It's a conversation starter that doesn't require a pitch.
06
L6I branded shirt on your back — every stop, no exceptions. You are the sample. In a military town, looking sharp and branded matters.

After the trip: Same day or next morning — text or email every warm contact. Reference something specific from the conversation. Send a quote link if you built one. Strike while it's fresh.
