Sales SOP
How we do it at Little 6 · Seven steps · Every time.
This tool exists so you can quote fast, quote right, and never leave a customer waiting. Follow these steps every time and we stay consistent — whether it's Matt, Lindsay, or a new rep on their first day.
Step 1
Set Up the Estimate
  • Open the Estimate Sheet before you calculate anything
  • Enter customer name, company, and email first — skip this and the PDF goes out blank
  • Enter your name in the Sales Rep field
  • Set the date — defaults to today, double-check it
Step 2
Build the Quote
  • Select a product from the sidebar or Home search
  • Enter quantity, size, and options — hit Calculate
  • Watch the traffic light:
✅ Green

Good to go. Add to estimate.

⚠️ Yellow

Cutting into margin. Confirm before quoting.

🚫 Red

Stop. Call Matt or Lindsay first.

  • Hit + Add to Estimate — repeat for every product
Step 3
Promo Products
  • Use the 🎁 Promo with Grace panel — not DC directly
  • Describe what the customer needs — quantity, budget, timeline
  • Check the ORDER FROM block — that's who you call
  • Always confirm pricing with the vendor before sending the PDF
Step 4
Send the Estimate
  • Hit ✉ Email Estimate — PDF goes to customer automatically
  • Copy goes to sales@little6llc.com so Matt and Lindsay know
  • Use Print if customer is in person · Copy to paste into a text
Step 5
Customer Approves
  • In-house orders — notify Lindsay to schedule production
  • Promo orders — call the vendor on the ORDER FROM block
  • Confirm artwork is ready before placing any order
Step 6
Discounts & Approvals
  • Standard discounts are built in — use them freely
  • Veterans and nonprofits get 15% off — select in the dropdown
  • Anything beyond standard needs Matt or Lindsay approval
Step 7
When You're Stuck
  • Pricing question — check traffic light first, then ask Lindsay
  • Product not in tool — Feedback button on Home, Matt adds it
  • Rush order — always confirm with Lindsay before promising
  • Something unusual — don't guess, call Matt

✅ When in doubt, underpromise and ask. A customer who waits is better than a customer who got the wrong price.
